On 9 July 2026, the EU revived mass chat-scanning rules through a procedural maneuver. Encrypted apps got a carve-out this time — because lawmakers chose to allow it, not because the architecture forced their hand. The next vote can go differently. Why "it's encrypted" isn't the end of the conversation

  • Encryption protects content, not metadata. Who you talk to, when, and how often is often the actual target of mass surveillance regimes — and most E2EE apps don't hide it.
  • A carve-out is a policy, not a wall. If a single company controls the app, a single legal order can flip its behavior for every user overnight.
  • Decentralized and self-hosted tools remove the single point of compulsion. There's no one server, no one company, no one court order that reaches everyone at once.
